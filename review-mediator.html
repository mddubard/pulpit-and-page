From the publisher

From animal sacrifices to kings: how mediators in the Bible point to final redemption through Jesus. How can sinful people approach a holy and just God? Humanity's only hope is Jesus, the "one mediator between God and men" (1 Timothy 2:5). This theme of mediation runs through the whole Old Testament — from animal sacrifices and altars to prophets, kings, and priests, each conveying the authority, power, and presence of God, and each a shadow of Christ.

In this rich, accessible study, Bible scholar Vern Poythress traces mediatorial figures throughout Scripture — people, angels, and objects such as the ark of the covenant — showing how they all culminate in Christ, the final prophet, king, and priest. Written for everyday Christians, it is also a useful resource for seminarians and pastors, giving readers a clearer view of the Old Testament, the greatness of Jesus, and their own calling to channel the word and power of God.

About the author. Vern S. Poythress (PhD, Harvard University; ThD, University of Stellenbosch) is Distinguished Professor of New Testament, Biblical Interpretation, and Systematic Theology at Westminster Theological Seminary in Philadelphia, where he has taught for four decades. He is the author of numerous books on biblical interpretation, language, and science.
