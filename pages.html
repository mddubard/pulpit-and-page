About Pulpit & Page

Who we are · What we do

Pulpit & Page exists to answer one recurring question: what new theology book is actually worth reading? Every week we track the newest and most notable releases across the Reformed, evangelical, Anglican, Baptist, and classic-Christian world, and organize them so you can find your next read in a couple of minutes instead of an afternoon.

What you'll find here

Three things, mainly. A continually updated Release Calendar of new and upcoming titles, organized by date and searchable by tradition and topic. Honest, concise reviews of the books worth your attention. And curated lists — the best new titles for pastors, the most anticipated releases of the season, notable academic works, and more.

Our approach

We read widely and recommend across traditions, with a home base in confessional Reformed and broadly evangelical theology. We aim to be fair to books we disagree with and honest about books we love. Our reviews reflect our own opinions and are never dictated by publishers or affected by whether a title earns us a commission.
